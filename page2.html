Ksi and the sidemen

The group has four YouTube channels, Sidemen, MoreSidemen, SidemenReacts and SidemenShorts across which it publishes a variety of videos including challenges, sketches and video game commentaries.[13][14][15] As of September 2021, the Sidemen channel has over 13.4 million subscribers and 3.1 billion views,[16] MoreSidemen has over 5.7 million subscribers and 1.9 billion views,[17] SidemenReacts has over 3.6 million subscribers and 835 million views,[18] and SidemenShorts has over 1.3 million subscribers and 330 million views.[19] On 18 June 2018, the group released a web television series titled The Sidemen Show, available exclusively on YouTube Premium.[20] It comprises seven 30-minute episodes filmed around the world alongside a number of celebrity guests.[21][22] In March 2020, the Sidemen released a twenty-minute YouTube video titled "#StayHome". The video featured the Sidemen and more than one hundred other YouTube video creators and other celebrities raising awareness of the UK's "stay at home" campaign which aimed to reduce the spread of COVID-19 in the UK.[23][24] All of the advertising revenue generated from the video was donated to the NHS

Since 2014, the group has sold and distributed Sidemen Clothing merchandise.[26][13] On 18 October 2016, the group released a book titled Sidemen: The Book, published by Coronet Books, and embarked on a UK-wide promotional tour.[27][28] A number one best-seller in the UK, the book sold 26,436 copies within the first three days of its release.[29][30] Sidemen Charity Football match 2018 at the Valley The group has hosted three football events to raise money for various charitable causes. The first match, in 2016, was held at St Mary's Stadium, Southampton, raising over £110,000 for the Saints Foundation.[31][32] The second and third matches were held at The Valley Stadium, London, raising £210,000 for the NSPCC and the Charlton Athletic Community Trust,[33][34] and £65,747 for the Young Minds Trust and the Charlton Athletic Community Trust,[35] respectively. In 2021, the Sidemen launched a subscription service known as Side+.[36] They also launched a restaurant chain known as Sides, and a vodka known as XIX Vodka
